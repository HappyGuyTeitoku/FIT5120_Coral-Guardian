Protect Our Water Resources by Reducing Nutrient Pollution

Learn how nutrient pollution impacts Melbourne's water quality and take action to safeguard our future.

Know about us

What is Nutrient Pollution?

Nutrient pollution occurs when an excess of nutrients, primarily nitrogen and phosphorus, enter water bodies.

These nutrients, often from sources like argricultural runoff, sewage and industrial waste, act as fertilizers in the water, leading to the overgrowth of algae. This excessive algae growth can harm water quality, disrupt aquatic ecosystems, and create dead zones where life cannot survive.

What we do

What can you do?

By making simple everyday choices, such as opting for eco-friendly products and reducing use of these products, you can significantly reduce nutrient pollution and help protect our water resources.

  • icon

    Use Eco-Friendly Products

    Choose phosphorus-free and low-nitrate detergents, soaps and cleaning products to minimise nutrient runoff into the sewage system.

  • icon

    Dispose of Waste Properly

    Dispose of household chemicals and medications at designated facilities.

  • icon

    Limit water usage

    Reduce the amount of waste water that waterplants have to process.

  • icon

    Share the knowledge with others

    Let your friends and family know about nutrient pollution, and get them to join your action.
